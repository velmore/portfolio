tory elmore

My greatest fascination with maps is the way in which the mapper affects the mapping. How does the way we view the world around us change when women map it? When children map it? When indigenous peoples map it? More importantly, how do we create opportunities for nontraditional mappers to start mapping?

My practical interests lie at the nexus of conservation and participatory GIS: working with citizen (spatial) scientists of all ages and background to collect, analyze, and visualize data. Learn more about my academic and professional background here.

I'm also in love with the aesthetics of maps. Mapstract was born one sweltering summer afternoon in my tiny Redlands sublet when I decided to start cutting up an old esri map book. Today I sell a full line of one-of-a-kind artwork and jewlery created entirely from maps. Check them out in my etsy shop
